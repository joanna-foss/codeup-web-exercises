Some Chess FAQs

How old is the game of chess?
The history of chess can be traced back nearly 1500 years, although its earliest origins are uncertain. The earliest predecessor of the game probably originated in India by the 7th century CE, from where it spread to Persia.
What is en passant?
En passant (French for 'in passing') is a special chess rule that gives pawns the option to capture a pawn which has just passed it.

How do I get better at chess?
No matter where you are in chess, you can always get better. And with the right habits and attitude, getting better at chess can be both fun and simple. To get better at chess, you will need to learn the rules, play a lot of games, review your play, practice puzzles, study the endgame, not waste time on openings, and double-check your moves.

What is a chess tourist?
GM Gary Kasparov famously referred to someone as a "chess tourist" in his quote about some of the participants in one of the FIDE knockout WCs. I.e. being obviously very good players but still missing something fundamental.
What is the difference between minor pieces and major pieces?
The major pieces are the queen and rooks. The minor pieces are the bishops and knights.
What are ratings in chess?
A chess rating system is a system used in chess to estimate the strength of a player, based on their performance versus other players.
  • 1200-1399: Beginner
  • 1400-1599: Average Club/Tournament Player
  • 1600-1799: Above Average Club/Tournament Player
  • 1800-1999: Strong Club Player
  • 2000-2199: Expert
  • 2200-2399: Master
  • 2400-2499: International Master
  • 2500 and above: Grandmaster
